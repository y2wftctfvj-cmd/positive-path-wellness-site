Clinical Framework

The Biopsychosocial Model

Sustainable outcomes require treating the whole person. Our approach integrates biological determinants, psychological patterns, and environmental contexts into a cohesive care plan.

Biological Determinants

Evidence-informed assessment and treatment planning that accounts for medical history, sleep, physiology, and (when indicated) careful psychopharmacology.

Psychological Patterns

Evidence-based modalities (CBT, DBT, MI) to improve emotional regulation, interrupt maladaptive patterns, and build durable coping skills.

Social Context

Recovery occurs within a system. We support navigation of family dynamics, workplace stressors, and community integration for sustainable outcomes.

Planned Services

Individual and group modalities utilizing empirically supported approaches to foster resilience and functional recovery.
